After a little over two years in operation, ESPN has said that they will not longer be accepting registrations for public API keys, and will be discontinuing all of their public APIs.

The ESPN public API has included access to headlines, athletes, teams, research, medals, scores, schedules, audio, video, photo, calendar, and a handful of other resources for the last two years.

The public API provided pretty clear guidance on developer terms of service, as well as branding guidelines dictating how developers can display resources obtained via the API.

The deprecation of the public ESPN APIs follow the lead of other big media industry APIs like the Netflix public API, which also closed down this year.
